OUR STORY

Who we are

Comms for a Cause: A Response to Authoritarian Narrative Warfare and Structural Invisibility

We are living in a time when the power of communications has never been more weaponized, or crucial to resistance.

Across the globe, authoritarian regimes understand something many movements have been forced to overlook: communications is power. Strategic communications is no longer an add on. It is the primary infrastructure through which undemocratic governments and institutions build dominance, manufacture legitimacy, spread disinformation, and suppress dissent.

In countries like India, Brazil, Hungary, and beyond, governments deploy highly coordinated comms strategies to create mythologies of control, smear activists, and neutralize opposition. At the same time, in places like Kashmir, Myanmar, China, and Russia, activists and grassroots movements are being cut off from the very tools they need to speak, organize, and be heard through internet shutdowns, social media censorship, algorithmic suppression, and legal crackdowns.

And it is not a coincidence that most major visibility platforms are owned by billionaires aligned with authoritarian institutions and powers. Their mutual gain is clear: the amplification of far-right ideology and capitalist surveillance in exchange for narrative control and cultural dominance. This results in a global digital ecosystem that prioritizes profit and control over truth and justice, making it nearly impossible for frontline movements to survive, let alone thrive.

Yet despite this reality, communications and digital security remain severely underfunded, overlooked, and deprioritized in philanthropy and progressive movement ecosystems. Comms is often treated as branding. Tech is treated as a luxury. Safety is treated as individual responsibility. And movements are collapsing as a result.

C4AC exists to change that.

Vision

We envision a world where grassroots movements, especially those led by queer, trans, Dalit, disabled, Indigenous, and feminist communities, have the tools, safety, and power to shape a just, equitable, and sustainable future.

We believe that strengthened movements strengthen movements. Every campaign, every action, every collective led by marginalized people is already contributing to a world free from violence, exploitation, discrimination, and climate catastrophe. But to do this work with power, safety, and assurance, movements need infrastructure that protects as much as it uplifts.

At Comms for a Cause, we imagine a future where movements can speak to donors, influence policy makers, confront anti-rights forces, organize across borders, and mobilize their communities without compromising their safety or values.A future where visibility does not come at the cost of protection

We are building toward deep-rooted, unapologetically political movements are secure, resourced, and able to thrive on their own terms. Movements where every strategy includes care, every plan includes protection, and every voice, no matter how marginalized, can be heard without fear.

Mission

Comms for a Cause builds infrastructure for movement safety, storytelling, and sustainability through a model we call Feminist, Holistic, and Radical Resource Redistribution (FHR3). Our mission is to redistribute not just funds, but strategic, technical, and emotional resources, meeting grassroots organizations where they are and walking alongside them as they implement bold, values-aligned visions.

We focus on two of the most underfunded yet essential pillars of movement work:

  • Storytelling led communications, to mobilize resources, shift discourse, and build alliances;
  • Digital security based tech infrastructure, to survive censorship, navigate risk, and sustain organizing without burnout or fear.

Through trauma-informed, strategic, creative, and justice tech–rooted accompaniment,we work alongside queer, trans, disabled, Dalit, neurodivergent, and feminist-led groups to build the communications and safety infrastructure they need to thrive and resist repression, violence, and authoritarianism.

Our Goals

Strengthen communications and digital security infrastructure for grassroots organizations who are the roots of global feminist movement building

We aim to equip frontline movements with the tools, skills, and systems to be visible without being vulnerable. This includes narrative clarity, secure communications, crisis response protocols, and sustainable internal infrastructure.

Embed feminist, trauma-informed, and justice-tech practices into movement building

You can easily identify interdependencies between different indicators, identify tendencies and prevent potential difficulties and problems.

Build collective narrative power systems against authoritarianism

We aim to position communications as infrastructure, creating systems that counter disinformation, resist disinformation campaigns, and strengthen democratic discourse through movement-rooted storytelling.

Redistribute resources and care to historically marginalized movements

Through our FHR3 model, we redistribute not only funding, but also time, tools, skills, and emotional resources to those most excluded from traditional funding pipelines, and yet are the most critical movement builders, to ensure their leadership is sustained and amplified.

Ensure feminist and movement voices shape global communications and digital security agendas.

We aim to accompany organizations into global convenings on communications and digital technology, bridging the gap between feminist movement and tech infrastructure. By ensuring they can directly engage funders, donors, and policymakers we strengthen people power.
